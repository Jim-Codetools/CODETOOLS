What is H1 Energy Efficiency?

Clause H1 of the New Zealand Building Code sets minimum requirements for energy efficiency and thermal performance in buildings. It defines how insulation, glazing, and other building envelope components must perform to reduce heat loss and improve indoor comfort.

H1/AS1 is an Acceptable Solution that provides a pathway for demonstrating compliance with Clause H1. It applies to housing and small buildings, and includes requirements for different climate zones across New Zealand.

Key Changes in H1/AS1 – 6th Edition Calculator

H1/AS1 Edition 6 introduces updates to improve flexibility, clarity, and alignment with current best practice. Key changes relevant to the calculator include:

  • The wall R-value used in the reference building heat loss equations has been updated to reflect the new requirements.
  • Wall framing is automatically assumed to be 38% of the total wall area.
  • Joinery specification options have been updated and expanded.
  • Slab functionality has been significantly enhanced to provide more options and to address buildings with a mix of cladding types, as well as cases where slab edge insulation does not extend around the entire floor perimeter.

These updates mean that results from the Edition 6 calculator reflect the most current acceptable solution under the New Zealand Building Code.
